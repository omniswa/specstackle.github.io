POCO X7 Pro Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 25, 2026

The POCO X7 Pro continues POCO’s legacy of aggressively priced, performance-focused smartphones. Originally launched in early 2025, it remains a dominant force in the 2026 mid-range market. Powered by the highly capable MediaTek Dimensity 8400 Ultra chipset, it effortlessly handles demanding games and heavy multitasking. Paired with a massive battery, ultra-fast 90W charging, and a vibrant 1.5K 120Hz AMOLED display, the X7 Pro punches well above its price tag, making it an excellent choice for users seeking flagship-level speed on a budget.

POCO X7 Pro Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Exceptional Price-to-Performance Ratio: The Dimensity 8400 Ultra offers flagship-grade processing power and handles top-tier gaming with ease.
  • Massive Battery: The 6,000 mAh (or 6,550 mAh in India) battery provides multi-day endurance for light users and all-day longevity for heavy gamers.
  • Premium Display: The 1.5K AMOLED panel with 3,200 nits of peak brightness and Dolby Vision support makes media consumption a joy.
  • Robust Durability: Securing an IP68/IP69 rating is rare for this price tier, providing substantial peace of mind against water and dust.

Cons:

  • Average Auxiliary Cameras: The 8MP ultrawide lens lacks detail, and ultrawide video recording is capped at 1080p at 30fps.
  • Software Bloatware: HyperOS 2 comes pre-installed with several third-party apps and occasional system ads.
  • No Expandable Storage or Headphone Jack: Users needing a 3.5mm jack or a microSD card slot will have to rely on adapters and cloud storage.

Buy or Skip?

Buy it if: You are a mobile gamer, power user, or media consumer on a budget. The POCO X7 Pro offers blazing fast speeds, phenomenal battery life, and a beautiful display that rivals phones twice its price.

Skip it if: You are a photography purist or prefer a clean, bloat-free Android software experience. While the 50MP main camera is decent, the secondary lenses are mediocre. Users who prioritize a streamlined OS without pre-installed apps should look at alternative options.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Redmi Note 14 Pro+: Made by POCO’s sister brand, this phone offers a very similar baseline experience but leans slightly more into premium design aesthetics and alternative camera tuning. It is a solid alternative if you prefer a different look.
  • Samsung Galaxy A56: If software is your main concern, the Galaxy A56 provides a cleaner interface (One UI) and longer guaranteed software support. However, it falls significantly behind the POCO X7 Pro in raw performance and charging speeds.
  • Nothing Phone (2a) Plus: This device targets the design-conscious crowd with its unique transparent back and Glyph interface. It offers a near-stock Android experience, though it cannot match the gaming capabilities of the Dimensity 8400 Ultra.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the POCO X7 Pro support wireless charging?

No, the POCO X7 Pro does not support wireless charging. However, it does feature blazing-fast 90W wired charging, which can replenish the battery to 50% in under 20 minutes.

Is the POCO X7 Pro good for gaming?

Yes, it is excellent for gaming. Powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 8400 Ultra and up to 12GB of LPDDR5X RAM, it smoothly runs demanding titles at high frame rates with excellent thermal management.

Is the POCO X7 Pro waterproof?

The device boasts an IP68 and IP69 rating, making it highly resistant to dust and water. It can survive submersion in fresh water and withstand high-pressure water jets, which is exceptionally rare for a mid-range phone.

Does the POCO X7 Pro have a headphone jack?

No, the device lacks a 3.5mm headphone jack. You will need to use a USB-C to 3.5mm adapter or rely on Bluetooth wireless headphones for audio.

How many software updates will the POCO X7 Pro get?

POCO promises up to 3 major Android OS updates and 4 years of security patches for the X7 Pro. Since it launches with Android 15, it should be supported up to Android 18.
