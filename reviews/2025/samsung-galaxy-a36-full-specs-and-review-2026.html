Samsung Galaxy A36 Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 15, 2026

The Samsung Galaxy A36 5G stands as a refined and highly reliable mid-range option, offering a subtle but meaningful evolution over its predecessor. Released in early 2025, it continues to hold its ground in 2026 by balancing flagship-tier software longevity with practical, everyday hardware. Equipped with a vivid 6.7-inch Super AMOLED display, a capable Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 processor, and an impressive six-year update policy, the Galaxy A36 is designed for users who want a device that will last. While it cuts a few corners—such as the removal of expandable storage and retaining a plastic frame—its stellar battery life, improved 45W charging speeds, and robust IP67 build make it a compelling choice for practical smartphone buyers.

Samsung Galaxy A36 Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Excellent Battery and Charging: The 5,000 mAh battery provides outstanding multi-day endurance for light users, and the upgrade to 45W charging is a welcome addition to the A-series.
  • Long-term Software Support: With 6 years of promised OS and security updates, this phone offers incredible longevity for the price.
  • Durable Build: Featuring an IP67 water and dust resistance rating alongside Gorilla Glass Victus+ on the front and back, it is tougher than many phones in its class.
  • Smooth Daily Performance: The Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 manages heat exceptionally well and easily handles multitasking, social media, and casual gaming.

Cons:

  • No Expandable Storage: Samsung has completely dropped the microSD card slot, forcing users to rely on internal storage or cloud backups.
  • Average Camera Consistency: While daylight shots are vibrant, low-light photography and ultra-wide lens consistency lag behind some competitors.
  • No Charger in the Box: To utilize the new 45W charging speeds, you will need to purchase a compatible power adapter separately.
  • Limited AI and Productivity: It lacks flagship-level AI features and does not support Samsung DeX.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Samsung Galaxy A36 for? The Galaxy A36 is tailor-made for practical buyers who prioritize longevity, battery life, and reliable software over raw processing power. If you want a dependable phone that will confidently handle web browsing, media consumption, and everyday apps for the next half-decade without needing an upgrade, this device is a perfect fit.

Who is it not for? Skip this phone if you are a heavy mobile gamer, an avid photographer, or a power user who relies on features like Samsung DeX. Additionally, users who need massive amounts of local expandable storage via a microSD card will need to look elsewhere.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Google Pixel 9a: The most direct rival to the A36. The Pixel 9a offers vastly superior camera performance and cleaner, more powerful AI software. However, it is slightly more expensive, starting at $499.
  • Samsung Galaxy A56: If you want to stick with Samsung but need a bit more graphical horsepower, better camera sensors, and a more premium metal build, the A56 is a worthy step up.
  • Samsung Galaxy A35 (Older Model): If you are on a tight budget, last year’s A35 offers a very similar display, camera array, and day-to-day experience for significantly less money.
  • Motorola Edge 60 Pro: An excellent alternative in a similar price bracket that offers faster charging, a sleeker aesthetic, and near-stock Android software.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Samsung Galaxy A36 have a headphone jack?

No, the Samsung Galaxy A36 does not feature a 3.5mm headphone jack. You will need to use wireless Bluetooth earbuds or purchase a USB-C dongle to connect traditional wired headphones.

Does the Samsung Galaxy A36 support wireless charging?

No, the Samsung Galaxy A36 does not support wireless charging. However, it does feature an upgraded 45W wired fast charging capability, allowing you to top up the 5,000 mAh battery relatively quickly.

Is the Samsung Galaxy A36 waterproof?

The Galaxy A36 comes with an IP67 rating for dust and water resistance. This means it can safely withstand being submerged in up to 1 meter of fresh water for up to 30 minutes, protecting it against accidental spills and rain.

Does the Samsung Galaxy A36 have an SD card slot?

Unlike its predecessors, the Samsung Galaxy A36 does not include a microSD card slot for expandable storage. Buyers will need to select the internal 128GB or 256GB storage capacity that best fits their needs.

How many software updates will the Galaxy A36 get?

Samsung has committed to providing up to 6 years of major Android OS and security updates for the Galaxy A36. This ensures your device will remain secure and compatible with new apps well into the next decade.
