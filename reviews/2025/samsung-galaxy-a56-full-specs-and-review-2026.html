Samsung Galaxy A56 Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 16, 2026

The Samsung Galaxy A56 marks a significant evolution in Samsung’s mid-range smartphone lineup. Released in early 2025, the device has remained a top contender in 2026 by offering flagship-grade features at a highly accessible price point. The most notable upgrades from its predecessor include a refined 7.4mm glass and aluminum chassis, a larger 6.7-inch Super AMOLED display hitting 1,900 nits of peak brightness, and the much-anticipated leap to 45W Super Fast Charging. Powered by the 4nm Exynos 1580 chipset, the Galaxy A56 introduces advanced AI capabilities into its camera processing and everyday tasks. Further cementing its value proposition, Samsung promises up to six generations of OS upgrades, making the Galaxy A56 an exceptionally reliable long-term investment.

Samsung Galaxy A56 Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Upgraded Charging Speed: The leap to 45W wired charging significantly reduces downtime, charging the device to 65% in just 30 minutes.
  • Vibrant Display: The 6.7-inch Super AMOLED panel with slim bezels and 1,900 nits of peak brightness is stunning for media consumption.
  • Premium Build: An aluminum frame paired with a glass back and an IP67 rating ensures durability and a flagship-like feel in hand.
  • Software Longevity: Samsung’s commitment to six generations of OS updates keeps the phone secure and feature-rich for years.
  • Capable Main Camera: The 50MP primary shooter with OIS produces sharp, well-balanced images enhanced by Galaxy AI.

Cons:

  • No Expandable Storage: The removal of the microSD card slot limits offline media storage options.
  • No Telephoto Lens: Missing out on a dedicated optical zoom lens leaves long-range photography somewhat compromised.
  • Charger Not Included: Buyers will need to purchase a 45W compatible charging brick separately.
  • Broad Footprint: With a width of 77.5mm, it can be slightly unwieldy for users with smaller hands seeking one-handed operation.

Buy or Skip?

Buy it if: You are looking for a reliable, future-proof mid-range smartphone. The Samsung Galaxy A56 is ideal for users who want premium aesthetics, a beautiful display for streaming, and a battery that finally supports faster 45W charging. The guarantee of six OS updates and integration into the broader Samsung Galaxy ecosystem makes it a highly sensible buy for the everyday user.

Skip it if: You are a heavy mobile gamer or a photography enthusiast who frequently relies on optical zoom. While the Exynos 1580 chip is competent and efficient, hardcore gamers might prefer performance-focused alternatives equipped with Snapdragon processors. Additionally, if you rely heavily on expandable local storage, the lack of a microSD slot will be a dealbreaker.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Google Pixel 8a / 9a: Google’s A-series remains the primary rival in the mid-range space. The Pixel offers superior still photography algorithms and day-one Android updates. However, the Galaxy A56 wins out with its larger 120Hz display, faster 45W charging, and overall superior battery endurance.
  • OnePlus 13R: A performance-heavy alternative at a similar price point. The OnePlus 13R provides a flagship-tier processor and blazing-fast charging speeds, making it the better pick for mobile gamers. In contrast, Samsung offers better software longevity, more consistent camera performance, and an IP67 durability rating.
  • Nothing Phone (2a) Plus: For those wanting a unique, conversational design, the Nothing Phone brings its distinctive Glyph interface and a bloat-free operating system. Still, the Galaxy A56 outshines it with a sturdier aluminum build, better long-term software support, and tighter AI integration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Samsung Galaxy A56 support wireless charging?

No, the Samsung Galaxy A56 does not support wireless charging. However, it compensates for this by offering 45W wired fast charging, which can quickly top up the 5,000 mAh battery to 65% in just 30 minutes.

Is the Samsung Galaxy A56 waterproof?

The Galaxy A56 features an IP67 rating for dust and water resistance. This means the device is fully protected against dust ingress and can withstand being submerged in up to 1 meter of fresh water for up to 30 minutes.

Does the Samsung Galaxy A56 have a headphone jack?

No, the Galaxy A56 does not include a 3.5mm headphone jack. Users will need to connect via wireless Bluetooth earbuds or use a USB Type-C to 3.5mm adapter for wired audio listening.

Can I expand the storage on the Samsung Galaxy A56?

Unlike many older models in the A-series lineup, the Galaxy A56 does not feature a microSD card slot for expandable storage. You will need to choose between the 128GB and 256GB internal storage variants or rely on cloud storage services.

What comes in the box with the Samsung Galaxy A56?

The retail box includes the Galaxy A56 smartphone, a USB Type-C to Type-C data cable, a SIM ejection pin, and a quick start guide. Samsung does not include a 45W power adapter or a protective case in the box, which must be purchased separately.
