Samsung Galaxy S25 Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 25, 2026

The Samsung Galaxy S25 continues the brand’s legacy of delivering premium compact smartphones without compromising on top-tier performance. Released in early 2025, this device packs the highly efficient Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite chipset and an upgraded standard 12GB of RAM, ensuring ultra-fast processing and seamless multitasking.

While it retains the familiar 6.2-inch dynamic AMOLED display and 4,000 mAh battery from its predecessor, the enhanced internals and deep integration of Samsung’s Galaxy AI make it a formidable pocket-sized powerhouse. It is designed for users who want flagship-level capabilities—including a highly capable triple-camera system—in a form factor that is easy to hold and operate with one hand.

Samsung Galaxy S25 Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Industry-leading processing power via the Snapdragon 8 Elite chipset.
  • Generous base memory with 12GB of RAM across all storage tiers.
  • Exceptionally bright and vibrant 120Hz AMOLED display.
  • Sleek, premium, and highly pocketable design.
  • Comprehensive suite of Galaxy AI features paired with long-term software support.

Cons:

  • No year-over-year upgrade to battery capacity (remains 4,000 mAh).
  • Wired charging speeds are still capped at a relatively slow 25W.
  • The overall design is almost identical to the Galaxy S24.
  • The base model still starts with only 128GB of internal storage.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Samsung Galaxy S25 for? The Galaxy S25 is ideal for users who prefer compact, lightweight smartphones but refuse to sacrifice flagship performance. If you want top-tier processing power, excellent cameras, and robust AI software in a device that easily fits in one hand or a small pocket, the S25 is a fantastic choice.

Who is it not for? Skip this device if you are a power user who prioritizes multi-day battery life or requires blazing-fast wired charging speeds. Additionally, current Samsung Galaxy S24 owners should skip the S25, as the design and hardware changes—aside from the processor and RAM bump—are too incremental to justify a direct upgrade.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Google Pixel 9: The Pixel 9 sits in the same price tier and offers a similarly compact form factor. While the Pixel provides slightly better battery life and arguably superior computational photography, the S25 wins out in raw processing power and an overall smoother display experience.
  • Apple iPhone 16: Apple’s base flagship is the most direct alternative for iOS users. Both phones feature compact dimensions and identical base starting prices, but the Galaxy S25 offers a 120Hz refresh rate display, significantly outperforming the iPhone 16's standard 60Hz screen.
  • Samsung Galaxy S25+: If you love the features of the base model but need more screen real estate and endurance, the S25+ is the most logical step up. It increases the display size to 6.7 inches, offers a much larger 4,900 mAh battery, and supports faster 45W wired charging.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Samsung Galaxy S25 come with a charger?

No, the Samsung Galaxy S25 does not include a charging brick in the box. It only comes with a USB-C to USB-C cable, meaning you will need to purchase a 25W-compatible adapter separately if you do not already own one.

Is the Samsung Galaxy S25 waterproof?

The Galaxy S25 features an IP68 rating for water and dust resistance. This signifies that the device can withstand being submerged in up to 1.5 meters of fresh water for up to 30 minutes without sustaining damage.

Does the Galaxy S25 have expandable storage?

No, the Samsung Galaxy S25 does not feature a microSD card slot for expandable storage. Buyers must choose between the 128GB, 256GB, or 512GB variants based on their long-term file and application needs.

What is the battery life of the Galaxy S25?

The device is equipped with a 4,000 mAh battery that typically provides around five to six hours of active screen time per day. While the physical battery capacity is unchanged from the previous generation, the highly efficient Snapdragon 8 Elite processor helps optimize overall power consumption.

Does the Galaxy S25 support wireless charging?

Yes, the Samsung Galaxy S25 supports up to 15W wireless charging when used with compatible charging pads. It also maintains support for reverse wireless charging, allowing you to charge smaller accessories like wireless earbuds directly from the back of the phone.
