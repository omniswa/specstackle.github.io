Samsung Galaxy S25 Plus Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 25, 2026

The Samsung Galaxy S25 Plus sits perfectly in the middle of Samsung’s flagship lineup, offering an ideal balance between the compact base model and the premium Ultra tier. Released in early 2025, it continues to hold its ground in 2026 as a powerhouse daily driver, bringing a spacious 6.7-inch QHD+ display, elite Snapdragon performance, and an impressive suite of One UI 7 Galaxy AI features. For users who want top-tier processing speeds, exceptional battery life, and a brilliant screen without paying the Ultra premium or dealing with a heavy footprint, the Galaxy S25 Plus is a formidable choice.

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • The Snapdragon 8 Elite chipset provides unmatched Android gaming and multitasking performance.
  • Gorgeous, bright 6.7-inch QHD+ screen that is excellent for media consumption and outdoor viewing.
  • Reliable all-day battery life with a 4,900 mAh capacity that occasionally outlasts the Ultra model in active testing.
  • Seven years of promised software and security updates ensure long-term usability.
  • Deeply integrated Galaxy AI tools in One UI 7 make photo editing, translation, and text generation effortless.

Cons:

  • Camera hardware remains largely unchanged from the previous two generations, relying heavily on software processing.
  • Charging speed is still capped at 45W wired, which lags behind many Chinese competitors.
  • Lacks the 5x optical zoom lens and S-Pen functionality found in the Ultra model.
  • The physical design feels very similar to past iterations, lacking fresh aesthetic innovations.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Samsung Galaxy S25 Plus for? This smartphone is perfect for users who want a large display and dependable battery life but prefer a lighter, more pocket-friendly design than the bulky Galaxy S25 Ultra. It is highly recommended for mobile gamers, heavy media consumers, and anyone looking to leverage advanced AI software tools natively on their device without sacrificing top-tier processing power.

Who is it not for? You should skip this device if you are currently using a Galaxy S24 Plus, as the year-over-year hardware upgrades are minimal and the camera system is virtually identical. It is also not the best choice for hardcore photography enthusiasts who demand extended optical zoom ranges; those users should look toward the Ultra variant or dedicated camera flagships instead.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Apple iPhone 16 Plus: Apple’s direct equivalent offers similar dimensions and stellar battery life, paired with iOS and the A18 chip. However, the Galaxy S25 Plus pulls ahead visually thanks to its 120Hz display, whereas the standard iPhone Plus models are historically capped at 60Hz.
  • Google Pixel 9 Pro: Google’s flagship rivals the S25 Plus with superior point-and-shoot camera processing and exclusive Pixel AI features. While the Pixel excels in still photography, Samsung edges it out in raw processing power and thermal management for heavy gaming.
  • Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra: If you need an integrated S-Pen stylus, an anti-reflective screen coating, and a far more versatile camera system (including a 200MP main and 5x optical telephoto lens), the Ultra is the logical step up from the Plus model.
  • OnePlus 13: A strong Android alternative that offers the exact same Snapdragon 8 Elite chip, significantly faster wired and wireless charging speeds, and a highly competitive camera array, usually launching at a lower price point.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Samsung Galaxy S25 Plus support wireless charging?

Yes, the Galaxy S25 Plus supports up to 15W wireless charging. It also supports reverse wireless charging, allowing you to charge accessories like your Galaxy Buds directly from the back of the phone.

Is the Galaxy S25 Plus waterproof?

The device comes with an IP68 rating for dust and water resistance. This means it can safely withstand being submerged in up to 1.5 meters of fresh water for up to 30 minutes.

Does the Galaxy S25 Plus have expandable storage?

No, the Galaxy S25 Plus does not include a microSD card slot for expandable storage. You will need to choose between the 256GB and 512GB internal storage options at the time of purchase, or rely on cloud storage.

What is the difference between the S25 Plus and the S25 Ultra?

While both feature the same Snapdragon 8 Elite processor, the Ultra model has a slightly larger, boxier design made with premium materials like titanium. The Ultra also includes a built-in S-Pen and a vastly superior camera system featuring a 200MP main sensor and a 5x optical zoom lens.

Does the Samsung Galaxy S25 Plus come with a charger in the box?

No, Samsung does not include a charging brick in the box. You will only receive a USB-C charging cable, meaning you must purchase a compatible 45W adapter separately to take advantage of its maximum fast-charging speeds.
