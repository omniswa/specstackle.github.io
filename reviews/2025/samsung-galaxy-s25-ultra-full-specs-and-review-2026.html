Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 27, 2026

The Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra, launched in early 2025, represents the peak of Samsung’s flagship smartphone lineup. Designed with a sleek, flat-edge titanium frame and a massive 6.9-inch Dynamic LTPO AMOLED 2X display, the device prioritizes both durability and visual excellence. Under the hood, the exclusive Snapdragon 8 Elite processor provides desktop-grade performance, optimizing heavily for on-device Galaxy AI operations without sacrificing battery efficiency. Its versatile quad-camera system—headlined by a 200MP main sensor and a newly upgraded 50MP ultra-wide lens—delivers exceptional photography and 8K video recording. While iterative in some hardware aspects, the S25 Ultra brings a highly refined software experience, though notable cost-saving measures, such as the removal of the S Pen’s Bluetooth functionality, mark a shift in its feature set.

Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Industry-leading display: The 6.9-inch AMOLED screen paired with an anti-reflective coating makes outdoor visibility class-leading.
  • Exceptional performance: The Snapdragon 8 Elite handles heavy multi-tasking, high-end gaming, and on-device AI tasks seamlessly.
  • Upgraded camera hardware: The shift to a 50MP ultra-wide sensor improves landscape and macro photography significantly.
  • Robust AI ecosystem: Deep integration of Galaxy AI tools makes everyday tasks, like photo editing and live translation, highly intuitive.
  • Refined ergonomics: Subtly rounded corners and a lighter frame make the massive phone more comfortable to hold.

Cons:

  • High price tag: Starting at $1,299.99, it requires a serious financial investment.
  • Downgraded S Pen: To cut costs, Samsung removed the Bluetooth functionality, meaning users lose Air Actions and the remote camera shutter feature.
  • Stagnant battery specs: The 5000 mAh battery capacity and 45W charging speed remain unchanged from previous generations.
  • Bulky size: Despite ergonomic tweaks, it remains a heavy, two-handed device.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra for? Power users, mobile photographers, and productivity enthusiasts will find the S25 Ultra highly capable. The inclusion of the S Pen, combined with efficient multi-tasking software and heavy AI integrations, makes it an ideal device for business professionals. It is also well-suited for users upgrading from an older generation (such as the Galaxy S22 Ultra or earlier) who want a massive leap in processing power, display quality, and camera versatility.

Who it is not for? Users looking for a compact, lightweight smartphone should avoid this 6.9-inch device. Furthermore, if you currently own a Galaxy S24 Ultra, the incremental upgrades to the camera and processor may not justify the $1,300 price tag. Finally, long-time Note or Ultra users who heavily relied on the S Pen’s Bluetooth remote features (like snapping photos from a distance) will be frustrated by its removal and should reconsider their upgrade.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Apple iPhone 16 Pro Max: The primary rival to the S25 Ultra, offering a comparable 6.9-inch display, the powerful A18 Pro chip, and unparalleled video recording capabilities. It lacks a stylus and heavy multi-window support but provides a more streamlined iOS experience.
  • Google Pixel 9 Pro XL: A slightly cheaper alternative with incredibly clean software and a focus on computational photography. The Pixel features its own robust suite of AI tools but falls short in raw gaming and processor performance compared to the Snapdragon 8 Elite.
  • Vivo X200 Pro: A major competitor in the Android camera space. It offers superior battery life and slightly faster charging, though it lacks the global availability, built-in stylus, and extended software support of the Samsung.
  • Samsung Galaxy S25 Plus: For users who want the same Snapdragon 8 Elite processor and Galaxy AI features for a lower price, the S25 Plus delivers a nearly identical core experience without the S Pen or the massive 200MP main camera.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra have a headphone jack?

No, the Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra does not include a 3.5mm headphone jack. Users will need to use wireless Bluetooth headphones or a USB-C to 3.5mm adapter for wired audio listening.

What colors does the Galaxy S25 Ultra come in?

The device is available in standard titanium finishes: Titanium Black, Titanium Gray, Titanium Silverblue, and Titanium Whitesilver. Samsung also offers exclusive online colors, including Titanium Jadegreen, Titanium Jetblack, and Titanium Pinkgold.

Is the S Pen on the S25 Ultra different from the S24 Ultra?

Yes, Samsung removed the Bluetooth functionality from the S25 Ultra’s S Pen as a cost-saving measure. This means the stylus no longer supports remote camera shutter features or “Air Actions”, though it retains standard drawing, writing, and navigation capabilities.

Does the Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra support expandable storage?

No, there is no microSD card slot on the Galaxy S25 Ultra for expandable storage. Buyers must choose between the fixed internal storage options of 256GB, 512GB, or 1TB at the time of purchase.

How fast does the Galaxy S25 Ultra charge?

The phone supports up to 45W wired fast charging, which can charge the device to approximately 65% in 30 minutes. It also supports up to 25W wireless charging via the newer Qi2 standard.
