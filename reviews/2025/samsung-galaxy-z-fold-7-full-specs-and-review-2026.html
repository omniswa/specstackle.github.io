Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 15, 2026

Released in July 2025, the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 represents a major design shift for Samsung’s book-style foldables. Prioritizing an ultra-slim footprint, the device measures an incredibly thin 4.2mm when unfolded and weighs just 215g. It features an expanded 8.0-inch Dynamic AMOLED 2X main screen and a wider, more traditional 6.5-inch cover display.

To achieve this extreme thinness, Samsung removed S Pen support, focusing instead on internal performance and camera hardware. The device is powered by the top-tier Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite for Galaxy processor and features the flagship 200MP main camera sensor previously reserved for the Ultra series. Paired with Android 16 and seven years of guaranteed software updates, the Z Fold 7 is built for heavy multitasking, on-device AI integration, and premium mobile photography.

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Impossibly thin 4.2mm unfolded profile and lightweight 215g chassis.
  • Flagship-grade 200MP main camera delivers excellent low-light and high-resolution photography.
  • Unmatched processing power with the Snapdragon 8 Elite chip.
  • Wider 6.5-inch cover display feels identical to using a standard smartphone.
  • Long-term reliability with an industry-leading seven years of OS and security updates.

Cons:

  • Extremely high $2,000 starting price.
  • The battery capacity remains stagnant at 4,400 mAh, yielding only average battery life.
  • 25W wired charging is remarkably slow for a modern flagship in this price category.
  • Loss of S Pen compatibility may alienate long-time Fold users.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 for? This device is targeted at early tech adopters, mobile gamers, and business professionals who want a tablet-sized screen in their pocket without the traditional foldable bulk. If you prioritize having top-tier cameras (200MP) and elite processing speed over legacy stylus functionality, the Galaxy Z Fold 7 is the most refined foldable Samsung has ever built.

Who is it not for? Skip the Z Fold 7 if you rely heavily on the S Pen for note-taking or drawing, as the hardware no longer supports it. It is also not recommended for budget-conscious buyers or heavy users who need a phone that charges from 0 to 100% in under an hour, as the 25W charging speed significantly lags behind the competition.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Google Pixel 9 Pro Fold: Google’s foldable offers a similarly thin design and highly optimized software for multitasking. However, its Tensor G4 processor is considerably weaker than the Snapdragon 8 Elite found in the Fold 7, making Samsung the better option for gaming and heavy workloads.
  • OnePlus Open 2: OnePlus continues to lead in battery technology. The Open 2 offers vastly superior wired charging speeds and a slightly larger battery capacity, making it a strong alternative for users frustrated by Samsung’s 25W charging limit.
  • Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra: For buyers who want the exact same Snapdragon 8 Elite processor, 200MP camera, and software ecosystem, but prefer a traditional slab phone. The Ultra retains the integrated S Pen and offers a larger battery for a lower price.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 support the S Pen?

No, the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 does not support the S Pen. Samsung removed the digitizer and stylus compatibility entirely to achieve the device’s ultra-thin 4.2mm unfolded design.

What is the battery capacity and charging speed of the Z Fold 7?

The device is equipped with a 4,400 mAh dual-cell battery, which is identical to the previous generation. It supports a maximum wired charging speed of 25W, alongside 15W wireless charging and 4.5W reverse wireless charging.

How much does the Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7 cost?

The base model of the Galaxy Z Fold 7, featuring 12GB of RAM and 256GB of storage, carries a starting MSRP of $2,000 in the US. Higher storage configurations, such as the 512GB and 1TB variants, increase the price accordingly.

Is the Galaxy Z Fold 7 waterproof?

The smartphone has an official IP48 dust and water resistance rating. This means it is protected against solid objects larger than 1mm in size and can safely survive being submerged in up to 1.5 meters of fresh water for 30 minutes.

What processor does the Galaxy Z Fold 7 use?

It is powered by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite for Galaxy chipset, built on an advanced 3nm process. This customized octa-core processor delivers high-end performance for demanding 3D gaming, multitasking, and localized AI features.
