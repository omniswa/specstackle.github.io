Apple iPhone 17e Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 19, 2026

Released in March 2026, the Apple iPhone 17e serves as the brand’s budget-friendly entry into the highly capable iPhone 17 lineup. Designed as a direct successor to the iPhone 16e, the device retains a familiar compact form factor while introducing flagship-level internal upgrades. It is powered by the latest-generation A19 chip, bringing full support for Apple Intelligence, and finally introduces MagSafe and Qi2 wireless charging to Apple’s affordable tier. Notably, the device starts at an aggressive $599 price point while doubling the base storage to 256GB, making it one of the most value-packed iPhones Apple has ever released. While it makes some compromises—such as a 60Hz display and a traditional notch instead of the Dynamic Island—it handles everyday performance and photography seamlessly.

Apple iPhone 17e Specifications

Wireless Charging MagSafe and Qi2 support (15W)

Build & Protection Aluminum frame, Glass back, Ceramic Shield 2, IP68 dust/water resistance
Dimensions / Weight 146.7 x 71.5 x 7.8 mm / ~170 grams
Notable Features Action Button, Face ID (Notch design). Note: Lacks Camera Control button
Colors Black, White, Soft Pink
Release Date March 11, 2026
Price $599 (256GB) / Higher for 512GB

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Exceptional Value: Starting at $599 with a generous 256GB of base storage is a massive win for consumers.
  • Flagship Performance: The A19 chip ensures future-proofing and smooth execution of AI-driven Apple Intelligence features.
  • MagSafe Included: Fixing a glaring omission from older budget models, the 17e fully supports Apple’s magnetic accessory ecosystem.
  • Solid Camera: The 48MP Fusion lens acts as an excellent point-and-shoot camera, utilizing a 2x crop to simulate a dual-lens setup.
  • Lightweight Design: At just 170g, it is noticeably lighter and more comfortable to use one-handed than the Pro models.

Cons:

  • Dated Display Tech: The screen remains locked at a 60Hz refresh rate, meaning it lacks the buttery smooth scrolling of 120Hz competitors.
  • Retains the Notch: There is no Dynamic Island here; the iPhone 17e continues to use the classic display notch.
  • No Secondary Lenses: You miss out on the versatility of a dedicated ultrawide or telephoto lens.
  • Missing Camera Control: Apple opted to include the Action Button but skipped the newer Camera Control key.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Apple iPhone 17e for? This phone is built for budget-conscious Apple fans and users upgrading from an iPhone 14, iPhone SE, or older. If you want a reliable everyday smartphone that nails the basics, takes great photos, and guarantees years of software updates via the A19 chip, the iPhone 17e is highly recommended. It is also the ideal entry point for users wanting to experience Apple Intelligence without spending flagship prices.

Who is it not for? Skip this device if you are a power user, mobile gamer, or photography enthusiast. The 60Hz display will feel sluggish if you are accustomed to ProMotion displays and the lack of an ultrawide lens limits creative photography. Furthermore, if you crave Apple’s latest aesthetic features like the Dynamic Island, you will need to look higher up the product stack.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Apple iPhone 17 (Standard): If you can stretch your budget, the standard iPhone 17 is the most direct step up. It provides the modernized Dynamic Island, a secondary ultrawide camera lens, and better overall graphical performance.
  • Google Pixel 10a: In the Android realm, Google’s mid-range Pixel 10a sits at a similar price point. While it doesn’t match the raw processing power of the A19 chip, the Pixel typically offers a 120Hz display and a more versatile camera array, making it a strong alternative for users not locked into the iOS ecosystem.
  • Apple iPhone 16e / iPhone SE: The iPhone 17e’s direct predecessors. If you currently own the 16e, the jump to the 17e is minimal in terms of physical design. However, for older SE users, the 17e is a monumental upgrade in battery life, display quality, and storage capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Apple iPhone 17e have MagSafe?

Yes, the iPhone 17e introduces MagSafe compatibility to Apple’s budget lineup. This allows users to attach magnetic chargers, wallets, and accessories, and it also supports fast wireless charging via the Qi2 standard.

What is the difference between the iPhone 17e and iPhone 17?

The standard iPhone 17 features the modern Dynamic Island, a multi-lens camera system with an ultrawide lens, and slightly more premium design elements. The iPhone 17e opts for a classic notch, a single rear camera, and a lower starting price.

Does the iPhone 17e have a Dynamic Island?

No, the iPhone 17e does not have the Dynamic Island. To keep costs down and differentiate the lineup, Apple retained the classic notch design for housing the Face ID sensors and front camera.

Does the iPhone 17e have the Action Button and Camera Control?

The iPhone 17e includes the customizable Action Button on the side frame. However, it does not feature the new Camera Control button found on higher-tier models.

What colors does the iPhone 17e come in?

The iPhone 17e is available in three elegant colors with a matte finish: Black, White, and a new Soft Pink.
