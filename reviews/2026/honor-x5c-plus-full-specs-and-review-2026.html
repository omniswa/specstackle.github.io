HONOR X5c Plus Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 21, 2026

The HONOR X5c Plus is an entry-level smartphone designed for users seeking reliability and longevity without breaking the bank. Running on the latest Android 15 via MagicOS 9.0, the device is built to handle everyday communication, social media, and media consumption with ease. Its standout feature is the massive 5260mAh battery, which easily stretches into multi-day usage for casual users. While it does not feature 5G connectivity or a high-end gaming processor, it compensates with a smooth 6.74-inch 90Hz display, eye-comfort technology, and a capable 50MP main camera. It is a highly practical device for those who prioritize battery life, essential functionalities, and modern software in a budget package.

HONOR X5c Plus Specifications

Feature Details
Display 6.74-inch TFT LCD, HD+ (720 x 1600 pixels), 90Hz refresh rate, ~260 ppi, Eye Comfort/Dynamic Dimming
Processor MediaTek Helio G81 (12nm), Octa-core (2x 2.0GHz Cortex-A75 + 6x 1.7GHz Cortex-A55)
GPU Mali-G52 MC2

Camera System (Front) 5MP (f/2.2 aperture). Up to 1080p @ 30fps video recording.
Battery/Charging 5260 mAh (typical) / 5130 mAh (rated), 15W wired charging
Connectivity 4G LTE, Dual Nano-SIM, Wi-Fi 5 (802.11 a/b/g/n/ac) dual-band, Bluetooth 5.1, GPS/GLONASS/GALILEO/BDS
Build and Ports 167.0 x 77.0 x 7.89 mm, 186g. Side-mounted fingerprint sensor, 3.5mm audio jack, USB Type-C, OTG.
Release Date September 2025 (Global), February/March 2026 (Selected Markets)
Price ~$125 USD (e.g., approx. ₱6,999 in the Philippines)

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Excellent Battery Life: The 5260mAh battery provides excellent screen-on time, easily lasting up to two days for light users.
  • Latest Software: It runs on Android 15 out of the box, ensuring up-to-date security and features.
  • Smooth Scrolling: The 90Hz refresh rate display makes UI navigation and social media scrolling feel snappy.
  • Practical Ports: It retains the beloved 3.5mm headphone jack and supports expandable microSD storage up to 1TB.

Cons:

  • Low Display Resolution: The 720p HD+ resolution is relatively low for a large 6.74-inch screen, meaning text and media will not be as sharp as on 1080p displays.
  • Slow Charging Speed: At only 15W, the massive battery takes roughly two hours to charge from 0 to 100%.
  • Entry-Level Performance: The Helio G81 processor will struggle with heavy multitasking and graphically demanding games.
  • No IP Rating: The device lacks an official IP rating for dust and water resistance.

Buy or Skip?

Buy it if: You are looking for a reliable, budget-friendly smartphone or a secondary device. It is a fantastic choice for students, elderly users, or those whose phone usage primarily involves messaging, calling, scrolling through social media, and watching videos.

Skip it if: You are a hardcore mobile gamer, a power user who constantly multitasks, or someone who relies on fast charging and 5G network speeds. If camera versatility (like ultrawide or macro lenses) and peak graphical performance are top priorities, you should skip this device and look into the mid-range market.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Xiaomi Redmi 13C: A direct competitor in the budget segment that features similar MediaTek performance and a 90Hz HD+ display. However, the Redmi 13C older operating systems out of the box compared to the X5c Plus’s Android 15.
  • Samsung Galaxy A06: Samsung’s entry-level offering provides a comparable 50MP camera and a similar price point. While the Samsung may offer better long-term software support guarantees, the HONOR X5c Plus has an edge in out-of-the-box battery capacity (5260mAh vs 5000mAh) and a higher 90Hz base refresh rate.
  • realme Note 50: Another hyper-budget alternative that features a 90Hz screen and IP54 dust and splash resistance. However, the realme Note 50 steps down slightly in camera resolution (13MP vs HONOR’s 50MP) and runs on older Android versions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the HONOR X5c Plus good for gaming?

The HONOR X5c Plus is suitable for casual games and well-optimized titles like Mobile Legends at lower settings. However, because it uses an entry-level MediaTek Helio G81 processor, it will struggle with frame drops and overheating on graphics-heavy games like Genshin Impact or Call of Duty Mobile.

Does the HONOR X5c Plus support 5G connectivity?

No, the HONOR X5c Plus is limited to 4G LTE networks. If you require faster 5G mobile data speeds for cloud gaming or rapid large-file downloads, you will need to look at higher-tier models.

How long does the battery of the HONOR X5c Plus last?

With its generous 5260mAh battery capacity, the phone easily lasts a day and a half to two days on a single charge under moderate usage. However, its 15W charging cap means a full recharge will take roughly two hours.

Does the HONOR X5c Plus have a fingerprint scanner?

Yes, the device features a side-mounted fingerprint scanner integrated directly into the power button for quick and easy unlocking. It also supports optical face recognition using the front-facing camera.

Can I expand the storage on the HONOR X5c Plus?

Yes, the HONOR X5c Plus includes a shared SIM slot that supports microSD expansion. You can expand the storage capacity up to 1TB, which is ideal for users who store massive amounts of photos, videos, and offline music.
