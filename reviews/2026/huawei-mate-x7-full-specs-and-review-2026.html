Huawei Mate X7 Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 14, 2026

The Huawei Mate X7 redefines the book-style foldable market with an incredibly thin 4.5mm unfolded profile and a robust aerospace-grade aluminum build. Powered by Huawei’s proprietary Kirin 9030 Pro chipset, it offers flagship-tier hardware paired with a stunning 8.0-inch LTPO OLED main display. Huawei continues to push mobile photography boundaries, outfitting the Mate X7 with an exceptional 50MP triple-camera system featuring RYYB sensors and a variable aperture. Although its lack of native Google Mobile Services and premium price tag remain substantial barriers for global buyers, it stands as a masterpiece of foldable hardware engineering.

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Incredibly thin 4.5mm unfolded chassis.
  • Bright, vibrant, and smooth 120Hz LTPO OLED displays.
  • Class-leading triple camera array with variable aperture and exceptional low-light capabilities.
  • Generous 5,600 mAh battery with fast 66W wired and 50W wireless charging.
  • Comprehensive IP58/IP59 ratings offer excellent water and dust resistance.

Cons:

  • Exorbitant global pricing of around €2,099 (~$2,463 USD).
  • Lacks native Google Mobile Services (GMS), complicating the app experience for global users.
  • The Kirin 9030 Pro chipset lags behind current competitors in raw performance and thermal management.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Huawei Mate X7 for? The Huawei Mate X7 is for foldable smartphone enthusiasts, tech early adopters, and mobile photography lovers who prioritize cutting-edge hardware over standard software ecosystems. If you value having one of the thinnest foldables on the market with a versatile, high-end camera system, this device is a compelling choice.

Who is it not for? Skip this device if you are deeply entrenched in the Google software ecosystem, as the lack of native Google apps requires significant workarounds. Additionally, mobile gamers or power users might find the Kirin 9030 Pro’s thermal throttling under load frustrating. Finally, the steep $2,400+ price tag makes it a difficult sell for budget-conscious buyers.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 7: The most direct alternative for global consumers. It offers comparable flagship build quality, significantly better chipset performance, seamless Google integration, and robust multitasking features like Samsung DeX, all at a generally lower starting price.
  • Honor Magic V5: Offers an exceptionally thin profile, strong battery life, and excellent OLED screens. Crucially, it ships with Google services out of the box and features a lower price point, making it a more practical alternative for global buyers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Huawei Mate X7 available globally?

Yes, Huawei launched the global variant of the Mate X7 in December 2025, reaching various international markets. Global units ship with EMUI 15.0 rather than the HarmonyOS 6.0 software found on the Chinese models.

Does the Huawei Mate X7 support Google Mobile Services?

No, the Huawei Mate X7 does not come with native Google Mobile Services (GMS) out of the box due to ongoing trade restrictions. Users will need to rely on Huawei’s AppGallery or utilize third-party workarounds to access their standard Google applications.

What processor does the Huawei Mate X7 use?

The device is powered by Huawei’s proprietary Kirin 9030 Pro octa-core chipset. While it delivers fluid day-to-day performance, testing indicates it trails behind modern flagship chips in raw processing power and thermal efficiency under heavy loads.

Is the Huawei Mate X7 waterproof?

The Huawei Mate X7 features impressive IP58 and IP59 ratings, providing robust protection against dust ingress and high-pressure water jets. This makes it one of the most durable foldable devices available regarding environmental protection.

How fast does the Huawei Mate X7 charge?

The smartphone packs a substantial 5,600 mAh battery that supports 66W wired fast charging. Additionally it offers 50W wireless charging and reverse wireless charging capabilities to conveniently top up your accessories on the go.
