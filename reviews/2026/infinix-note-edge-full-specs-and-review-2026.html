Infinix NOTE Edge Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 13, 2026

The Infinix NOTE Edge is a premium-looking mid-range smartphone officially launched globally in early 2026. Its biggest engineering feat is packing a massive 6,500mAh silicon-carbon battery into an ultra-slim 7.2mm body. Functioning as the debut device for the MediaTek Dimensity 7100 chipset, it offers highly competent day-to-day performance paired with a vibrant 6.78-inch 1.5K 3D curved AMOLED display. With an IP65 rating, JBL-tuned stereo speakers, and extensive software support promised by the manufacturer, the NOTE Edge is positioned as a feature-rich, high-endurance device at an accessible price point.

Infinix NOTE Edge Specifications

Feature Details
Display 6.78-inch 3D Curved AMOLED, 1.5K resolution (1208 x 2644), 120Hz refresh rate, 4,500 nits peak brightness, 2160Hz PWM Dimming, Corning Gorilla Glass 7i

Build & Design 7.2mm ultra-slim profile, IP65 dust and water resistance. Colors: Lunar Titanium, Silk Green (textured), Stellar Blue, Shadow Black

Other Notable Features Under-display fingerprint scanner, JBL-tuned dual stereo speakers, customizable One-Tap AI button (Folax AI)

Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Exceptional Battery Life: The 6,500mAh silicon-carbon battery is one of the largest in its class, capable of multi-day use.
  • Ultra-Slim Design: Despite the huge battery, the phone maintains a sleek 7.2mm profile and an ergonomic 3D curved screen.
  • Bright, High-Resolution Display: The 1.5K AMOLED panel reaching up to 4,500 nits peak brightness outshines many competitors in this price bracket.
  • Generous Software Support: Infinix promises 3 major OS updates and 5 years of security patches, a significant step up for the brand.

Cons:

  • Lack of OIS: The primary 50MP camera lacks Optical Image Stabilization, which limits its performance in low-light environments and action photography.
  • Slower Storage Protocol: The use of UFS 2.2 storage is a bit dated for 2026, leading to slower app installation and file transfer times compared to devices with UFS 3.1 or 4.0.
  • No Ultrawide Lens: The rear camera module relies on an unspecified auxiliary lens instead of a dedicated ultrawide or telephoto camera.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Infinix NOTE Edge for? This smartphone is highly recommended for heavy media consumers, gig-economy workers, and power users who prioritize battery life above all else. If you want a device that looks premium, features a vivid curved display for watching videos, and can easily survive two days off the charger, this is an excellent buy.

Who is it not for? Skip this device if you are a mobile photography enthusiast. The lack of OIS and the absence of a dedicated ultrawide lens make it a weak contender for taking highly detailed low-light photos or sweeping landscape shots. Hardcore gamers demanding maximum graphics performance may also want to look for a device featuring a flagship-tier processor.

Competitors and Alternatives

When shopping in the $200–$250 mid-range category, the Infinix NOTE Edge faces strict competition.

  • Redmi Note 15: The Redmi Note 15 is its closest rival. While it has a smaller battery, the Redmi provides a superior camera experience with a wider field of view, superior daylight detail, and overall better exposure control.
  • Nothing Phone (3a) Lite: A strong alternative for users who prefer clean, bloatware-free software. However, the Nothing Phone (3a) Lite features a lower-resolution 1080p screen and a lower peak brightness compared to the NOTE Edge’s 1.5K display.
  • Samsung Galaxy A-Series (A36): For those who prefer brand reliability and superior camera stabilization, Samsung’s mid-range offerings are a safer bet, albeit they come with significantly smaller batteries and slower charging speeds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Infinix NOTE Edge have a headphone jack?

Not specified by the manufacturer. However, given its ultra-slim 7.2mm profile, it is highly unlikely to feature a 3.5mm audio port, pushing users toward Bluetooth or USB-C audio solutions.

Is the Infinix NOTE Edge good for gaming?

Yes, it is highly capable for casual and moderate gaming. The MediaTek Dimensity 7100 chipset scores over 750,000 on AnTuTu and can sustain 60 to 90fps on popular competitive titles.

Does it support wireless charging?

No, the Infinix NOTE Edge does not support wireless charging. It relies strictly on its 45W fast wired charging to top up the massive 6,500mAh battery.

Is the Infinix NOTE Edge waterproof?

The device comes with an IP65 rating. This means it is fully protected against dust and can withstand low-pressure water jets (like rain or splashes), but it is not fully waterproof and should not be submerged in water.

Does the Infinix NOTE Edge have expandable storage?

There are conflicting reports on this feature, but official data generally points to no microSD card expansion. Buyers should choose their storage variant (128GB or 256GB) carefully at the time of purchase.
