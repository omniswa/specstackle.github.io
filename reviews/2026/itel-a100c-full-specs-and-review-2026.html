itel A100C Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 21, 2026

The itel A100C is an ultra-budget smartphone designed to offer maximum durability and basic functionality at a highly accessible price point. Officially launched in the Philippines in March 2026, the device sets itself apart in the entry-level market with its MIL-STD-810H compliance, offering reliable drop resistance up to 1.2 meters. Running on the lightweight Android 15 (Go Edition) and powered by a UNISOC T7100 chipset, it is tailored for smooth daily operation rather than heavy processing. With a 6.6-inch 90Hz display, dual SIM 4G connectivity, and a robust 5000 mAh battery, the itel A100C presents a practical and long-lasting mobile experience for students, first-time smartphone users, and those needing a rugged secondary device.

itel A100C Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Military-Grade Durability: The MIL-STD-810H certification makes the phone exceptionally resistant to accidental drops and daily wear and tear.
  • Smooth Scrolling: A 90Hz refresh rate is a rare and welcome addition in this price bracket, making UI navigation feel smoother.
  • Exceptional Battery Life: A 5000 mAh battery combined with an efficient Go Edition OS easily yields well over a day of moderate usage.
  • Value for Money: Starting at roughly $60, it delivers great core essentials without breaking the bank.

Cons:

  • Low Processing Power: The UNISOC T7100 chipset is meant strictly for basic tasks; it will struggle with gaming and heavy multitasking.
  • Slow Charging: With only 10W charging speeds, replenishing the large 5000 mAh battery takes a significant amount of time.
  • Basic Camera Quality: The 8MP main camera and 5MP selfie shooter offer passable daylight shots but perform poorly in low-light environments.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the itel A100C for? Buy this device if you are looking for an ultra-affordable, highly durable smartphone. It is an excellent choice for first-time smartphone buyers, young students, fleet workers needing a durable communication device, or anyone who wants a reliable secondary phone. Its drop-resistant build and large battery make it ideal for dependable, no-frills daily use.

Who is it not for? Skip this device if you are a mobile gamer, heavy multitasker, or photography enthusiast. The basic entry-level processor, limited RAM, and minimal camera setup are not designed to handle demanding apps, high-end graphics, or professional-looking social media photography. Power users should look for devices in higher price brackets.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Tecno Spark 40C: Positioned slightly higher in price, this alternative offers a MediaTek Helio G81 processor and a 90Hz display, making it a better option for users seeking slightly more graphical and processing performance in the budget space.
  • Realme Note 70: A direct competitor in the same price range, the Note 70 boasts a larger 6300 mAh battery and a Unisoc T7250 chipset, prioritizing multi-day battery life over drop-resistance durability.
  • Samsung Galaxy A07 4G: If brand reliability and software longevity are a priority, the Galaxy A07 4G features a more robust MediaTek Helio G99 processor. However, you will have to pay a noticeably higher price compared to the itel A100C.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the itel A100C support 5G connectivity?

No, the itel A100C does not support 5G networks. It relies on standard 4G LTE, which is customary and expected for an ultra-budget smartphone in this price segment.

Is the itel A100C good for gaming?

The device is not built for heavy gaming. Powered by a UNISOC T7100 chipset and utilizing Android 15 Go Edition, it is only capable of handling lightweight, casual games and will struggle with graphically demanding titles.

Is the itel A100C waterproof?

No, the itel A100C is not waterproof and lacks an official IP rating for submersion. However, it is highly durable, featuring a MIL-STD-810H compliance that protects it from accidental drops of up to 1.2 meters.

Does the itel A100C have a fingerprint scanner?

Yes, the smartphone is equipped with a side-mounted fingerprint scanner. This sensor is built directly into the power button, allowing for fast and secure unlocking of the device.

What is included inside the itel A100C box?

The retail packaging typically includes the itel A100C handset, a compatible charging brick, a USB Type-C cable, and standard quick-start documentation. It provides everything you need to start using the phone immediately right out of the box.
