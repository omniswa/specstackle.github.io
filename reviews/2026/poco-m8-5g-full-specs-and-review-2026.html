POCO M8 5G Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 13, 2026

The POCO M8 5G, officially released in January 2026, aims to deliver remarkable value in the highly competitive budget-to-midrange smartphone market. Stepping up significantly from its predecessors, the device trades standard LCDs for a brilliant 6.77-inch 3D curved AMOLED display capable of hitting an impressive 3,200 nits of peak brightness. Paired with a sleek 7.35mm chassis and an IP65/IP66 dust and splash resistance rating, the phone looks and feels distinctly more premium than its price tag suggests.

Under the hood, the Qualcomm Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 processor provides reliable performance for daily tasks and casual gaming. POCO strongly emphasizes longevity this year, equipping the M8 5G with a massive 5,520mAh silicon-carbon battery and committing to four years of OS updates alongside six years of security patches. While the camera array is somewhat modest and lacks an ultrawide lens, the POCO M8 5G stands out as an excellent workhorse for budget-conscious buyers seeking dependable battery life, robust software support, and a stunning screen.

POCO M8 5G Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Outstanding 120Hz curved AMOLED screen with exceptional peak brightness (3,200 nits).
  • Class-leading battery life thanks to a large 5,520mAh capacity and efficient 45W charging.
  • Durable build quality featuring IP65/IP66 ratings and MIL-STD-810H military-grade testing.
  • Generous, long-term software support (4 major OS updates and 6 years of security patches).
  • Dependable everyday performance with excellent thermal management.

Cons:

  • No ultrawide camera, limiting photography versatility.
  • The 4K video recording lacks optical image stabilization (OIS).
  • Out-of-the-box HyperOS 2 software includes noticeable bloatware.

Buy or Skip?

Who this device is for: The POCO M8 5G is highly recommended for media consumers, students, and budget shoppers who prioritize battery life, screen quality, and physical durability. It is an ideal daily driver for users who want an affordable phone that easily lasts a full day of scrolling, video streaming, and light multitasking without running out of juice.

Who it is not for: Skip this device if you are a heavy mobile gamer or an avid mobile photographer. The lack of an ultrawide camera and average video stabilization holds it back from being a content creator’s tool. Additionally, users who want to play graphically demanding 3D games on maximum settings should look toward devices equipped with flagship-grade processors.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Redmi Note 15 5G: Sharing nearly identical internal specifications under the broader Xiaomi umbrella, the Redmi Note 15 5G acts as a direct sibling rival. It appeals more to users who prefer Redmi’s distinct design aesthetics over POCO’s styling, though performance and battery life are practically indistinguishable.
  • POCO M8 Pro 5G: For buyers willing to spend a bit more, the M8 Pro 5G features a massive 6,500mAh battery, much faster 100W Hyper Charging, and slightly better camera tuning. It is the logical upgrade if your budget allows for it.
  • Nothing Phone (2a) / CMF Phone 1: These alternatives dominate the same budget tier by offering cleaner, bloatware-free Android experiences and unique external designs. However, they generally fall short of the M8 5G’s extreme peak display brightness and extensive battery capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the POCO M8 5G support expandable storage?

Yes, the POCO M8 5G features a hybrid dual SIM tray that allows you to expand your storage. You can easily insert a microSD card to add up to 1TB or 2TB of extra space for your photos and media.

Is the POCO M8 5G water-resistant?

The device carries IP65 and IP66 ratings, meaning it is highly resistant to dust and can withstand strong water jets and heavy splashes. However, it is not fully waterproof, so you should avoid completely submerging the phone in water.

Does the POCO M8 5G have a headphone jack?

No, the POCO M8 5G does not come with a traditional 3.5mm audio jack. You will need to use wireless Bluetooth earbuds or plug in a USB Type-C audio adapter to listen privately.

How many software updates will the POCO M8 5G receive?

POCO promises excellent software longevity for this model, guaranteeing 4 years of major Android OS upgrades. Additionally, the phone is slated to receive 6 years of security patches, keeping your data secure well into the future.

Is the POCO M8 5G good for gaming?

The phone handles casual and moderately demanding games quite well thanks to its Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 chip and ultra-responsive 120Hz display. For more intensive 3D titles, you may need to drop the graphics settings to low or medium to maintain a consistently smooth frame rate.
