vivo iQOO Z11x 5G Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 17, 2026

The vivo iQOO Z11x 5G, officially launched in March 2026, is a highly capable budget-friendly smartphone designed for consumers who demand extreme battery longevity. Centered around an ultra-massive 7200mAh battery and a power-efficient 4nm MediaTek Dimensity 7400 Turbo processor, it handles prolonged gaming and heavy daily use with ease. To separate itself from typical mid-range offerings, the device brings flagship-grade durability with an IP68 and IP69+ rating for dust and high-pressure water resistance. While it utilizes an IPS LCD screen instead of an AMOLED panel, it delivers a smooth 120Hz refresh rate, 1200 nits of peak brightness, and excellent overall value for the price point.

vivo iQOO Z11x 5G Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Class-leading 7200mAh battery provides outstanding multi-day usage without needing a top-up.
  • Remarkable IP68 and IP69+ water and dust resistance, a rarity in this price bracket.
  • Solid mid-range performance with the Dimensity 7400 Turbo chipset and fast UFS 3.1 storage.
  • Features community-requested hardware like a 3.5mm headphone jack and stereo speakers.
  • Generous software support commitment (2 years of OS updates and 4 years of security patches).

Cons:

  • Relies on an IPS LCD screen rather than offering the vibrant contrast of an AMOLED panel.
  • Unavoidably thick and heavy (219 grams) due to the massive battery capacity.
  • 44W charging speed is relatively slow for a 7200mAh battery, leading to longer charging times.
  • Lacks an ultrawide camera, using a basic 2MP secondary lens instead.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the vivo iQOO Z11x 5G for? This smartphone is highly recommended for gig economy workers, budget gamers, and heavy users who suffer from battery anxiety. If you frequently spend long hours outdoors or away from a charger, the 7200mAh battery and power-efficient chip will easily get you through the day. The IP69+ rating also makes it incredibly appealing to individuals working in demanding physical environments where water and dust exposure are constant threats.

Who is it not for? Skip this device if you are a mobile photography enthusiast or someone who prefers slim, lightweight hardware. Weighing 219 grams, it is noticeably heavy in the pocket. Additionally, the camera system is very basic—the absence of an ultrawide lens limits your photographic versatility, and media purists will likely want to look for alternatives featuring AMOLED displays.

Competitors and Alternatives

When evaluating the iQOO Z11x 5G in the sub-$250 category, consider the following alternatives:

  • Moto G86 Power: Motorola’s latest entry in its “Power” series is the closest competitor regarding battery life. While the Moto matches up reasonably well in battery endurance, the iQOO Z11x 5G generally offers slightly better raw processing power for gaming thanks to the Dimensity 7400 Turbo.
  • POCO M8: A direct rival focusing on performance and gaming. It features an aggressive processor setup but caps out at standard battery capacities, losing the endurance race to the iQOO.
  • realme 14T: If vivid display quality is your priority, the realme 14T offers an OLED/AMOLED experience in a similar price bracket. However, you will have to sacrifice the extreme IP69+ durability ratings and the gargantuan battery found in the iQOO.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the price of the vivo iQOO Z11x 5G?

The vivo iQOO Z11x 5G starts at approximately $230 (Rs. 18,999) for the base model featuring 6GB of RAM and 128GB of storage. Higher configurations, such as the 8GB/256GB variant, peak at roughly $280 (Rs. 22,999).

Does the vivo iQOO Z11x 5G have an AMOLED screen?

No, the device utilizes a 6.76-inch IPS LCD panel. While it lacks the infinite contrast of an AMOLED screen, it compensates with a smooth 120Hz refresh rate and a peak brightness of 1200 nits to maintain good outdoor visibility.

Is the vivo iQOO Z11x 5G waterproof?

Yes, the smartphone is remarkably well-protected against the elements. It carries both an IP68 and an IP69+ rating, meaning it can withstand prolonged submersion in water as well as high-temperature, high-pressure water jets.

Can I expand the storage on the iQOO Z11x 5G?

No, the device does not feature a dedicated or shared microSD card slot for expandable storage. Buyers will need to select between the 128GB and 256GB internal storage configurations at the time of purchase depending on their needs.

Is the iQOO Z11x 5G good for gaming?

Yes, it is highly capable for budget gaming. The combination of the MediaTek Dimensity 7400 Turbo processor, fast UFS 3.1 storage speeds, and the massive 7200mAh battery ensures stable frame rates and long, uninterrupted gaming sessions.
