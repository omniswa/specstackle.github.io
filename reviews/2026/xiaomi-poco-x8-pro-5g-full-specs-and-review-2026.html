Xiaomi Poco X8 Pro 5G Full Specs and Review (2026)

Published on March 20, 2026

The Xiaomi Poco X8 Pro 5G arrives in 2026 as an aggressive flagship killer aimed squarely at gamers and power users. Featuring a MediaTek Dimensity 8500-Ultra chipset and a massive 6500mAh battery paired with 100W ultra-fast charging, the device ensures long-lasting peak performance. Its 6.59-inch 1.5K AMOLED display pushes up to 3500 nits of peak brightness, all protected by Gorilla Glass 7i and a top-tier IP69K durability rating. While its camera setup is relatively modest compared to photography-centric rivals, the staggering hardware and overall value make it one of the most compelling mid-range smartphones of the year.

Xiaomi Poco X8 Pro 5G Pros & Cons

Pros:

  • Exceptional Battery Life: The industry-leading 6500mAh battery easily provides up to a day and a half of heavy use, supported by blazing 100W fast charging.
  • Flagship Durability: The inclusion of IP69K water and dust resistance, along with an aluminum frame, is incredibly rare in this price bracket.
  • Stunning Display: The 1.5K AMOLED screen with up to 3500 nits of peak brightness delivers vibrant, immersive visuals under direct sunlight.
  • High-End Performance: The Dimensity 8500-Ultra processor handles demanding multitasking and heavy gaming with ease.

Cons:

  • Average Camera Versatility: The secondary camera is restricted to a basic 8MP ultrawide lens, and there is no dedicated telephoto sensor.
  • Software Bloat: Xiaomi’s HyperOS 3 comes with several pre-installed applications that clutter the out-of-the-box experience.
  • Hefty Build: Accommodating a 6500mAh battery adds to the physical weight (over 201g) and thickness of the phone.

Buy or Skip?

Who is the Xiaomi Poco X8 Pro 5G for? This smartphone is purpose-built for mobile gamers, heavy media consumers, and battery-life enthusiasts. If you want a device that can handle long, intense gaming sessions without needing to be plugged in constantly, the Dimensity 8500-Ultra and 6500mAh battery combination is an absolute steal at this price point.

Who is it not for? Skip this device if your primary focus is mobile photography or videography. While the 50MP Sony IMX882 main sensor captures good daylight shots, the overall camera system struggles to match photography-focused flagships. Additionally, users who prefer featherlight, ultra-compact phones might find this device a bit too bulky for their liking.

Competitors and Alternatives

  • Redmi Note 16 Pro+: A close sibling under the Xiaomi umbrella. It shares similar display properties but generally offers higher-resolution camera sensors and a slightly more refined aesthetic, making it a better pick for casual photographers.
  • OnePlus Nord Series (Nord 5/6): Provides a much cleaner, bloat-free software experience via OxygenOS and well-balanced camera tuning. However, it falls significantly short of the Poco’s 6500mAh battery capacity.
  • Samsung Galaxy A56 / A57: The ideal alternative for users prioritizing long-term software support, a refined user interface, and consistent camera processing. The trade-off is that Samsung’s mid-range devices offer noticeably slower charging speeds and a weaker processor for gaming.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Poco X8 Pro 5G support wireless charging?

No, the Poco X8 Pro 5G does not support wireless charging. It compensates by offering 100W ultra-fast wired charging and 27W reverse wired charging to quickly power up your accessories.

Is the Xiaomi Poco X8 Pro 5G waterproof?

Yes, the smartphone offers superior durability with IP66, IP68, and IP69K ratings. This means it is highly protected against total dust ingress, continuous water immersion, and even high-pressure, high-temperature water jets.

What processor does the Poco X8 Pro 5G use?

The device is powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 8500-Ultra, built on an efficient 4nm architecture. This chipset is highly optimized for multitasking, energy efficiency, and high-end mobile gaming.

Does the Poco X8 Pro 5G have an expandable SD card slot?

No, the device does not support expandable storage via a microSD card. Fortunately, the base models offer generous memory configurations starting at 256GB and going up to 512GB of fast UFS 4.0 storage.

Is the Poco X8 Pro 5G good for gaming?

Yes, it is an excellent choice for gamers. The combination of the Dimensity 8500-Ultra, up to 12GB of LPDDR5X RAM, a smooth 120Hz AMOLED display, and a massive 6500mAh battery allows for long, uninterrupted gaming sessions at high frame rates.
